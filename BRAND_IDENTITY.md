# TimesLeft Brand Identity — "The Void"

## 1. Brand Essence

TimesLeft confronts you with a number. Not gently — directly. The design exists to make that number impossible to ignore.

**The rule: The number IS the design. Everything else gets out of the way.**

### Voice
- Honest, intimate, still
- Second person and names ("you", "Mom") — never abstractions
- No exclamation marks, no urgency language, no corporate-speak
- The quiet confidence of someone who has thought deeply about time

### Emotional Arc
```
Onboarding:  Curious → Vulnerable → Gut-punch → Resolved
Daily use:   Grounded → Reminded → Purposeful
Sharing:     Reflective → "What IS that?" → Connected
```

## 2. Color Palette

Three colors. That's it.

| Role | Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Background** | Void (deep navy) | `#0C0E14` | 0.047, 0.055, 0.078 | All backgrounds. Darkness with depth — not dead black. |
| **Text** | Bone (warm beige) | `#E8E0D4` | 0.91, 0.88, 0.83 | Primary text. Human warmth, not clinical white. |
| **Accent** | Copper (oxidized) | `#B87346` | 0.72, 0.45, 0.27 | Exclusively for "what's left" — remaining visits, CTAs, hero numbers, active states. |

### Supporting Tones (derived, not independent)

| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| `tlTextSecondary` | `#6B6D78` | Muted warm gray-blue. Captions, supporting info. |
| `tlTextTertiary` | `#3A3C44` | Barely visible. Section labels, watermarks. |
| `tlSurface` | `#12141C` | Subtle elevation for sections needing separation. |
| `tlDivider` | `#1E2030` | Hairline separators. |
| `tlPast` | `#1A1C26` | Grid cells for time already spent. Nearly invisible. |

### Relationship Colors (copper/bronze family)
Subtle metallic variations — warm but restrained:
- Parent: `#C78559` | Partner: `#BF7A73` | Friend: `#C29961`
- Sibling: `#B38061` | Child: `#D19E73` | Grandparent: `#997A61` | Other: `#8C8578`

### Color Rules
- **Copper means ONE thing**: time remaining / what's left. If it's copper, it's about what you still have.
- **No other accent colors.** No green for insights, no red for urgency. Copper or nothing.
- **Dark mode only.** This subject doesn't need sunshine. The void is always the canvas.

## 3. Typography

Two voices. That's it.

| Tier | Font | Weight | Usage |
|------|------|--------|-------|
| **Emotional numbers** | New York (system serif) | Light | Hero reveals (120pt), display numbers (72pt), feature numbers (48pt), stat values (32pt), occasion counts |
| **Everything else** | SF Pro (system) | Various | Titles, body, labels, buttons, captions, navigation |

### Type Scale
- Hero number: 120pt serif light (the reveal)
- Display number: 72pt serif light (Time Portrait)
- Feature number: 48pt serif light (dashboard hero)
- Stat number: 32pt serif light (card values)
- Title: 28pt SF semibold
- Body: 17pt SF regular
- Label: 11pt SF medium, ALL-CAPS, tracked +1.5

### Why Serif for Numbers
A serif number at 120pt on dark feels weighty, consequential — "this matters." Sans-serif at the same size reads as data. We want gravitas, not dashboard.

### What Was Removed
- Caveat (hand-drawn font) — identified as AI-generated design tell
- System .rounded — too friendly for the subject matter
- Monospaced digit variants — unnecessary complexity

## 4. Layout Philosophy

**No cards. No shadows. No rounded corners.**

Content floats in the void. Separation through:
- **Generous vertical spacing** (24-32pt between sections)
- **Hairline dividers** (0.5pt, `tlDivider` color)
- **Surface elevation** (subtle `tlSurface` background for grouped content)
- **ALL-CAPS tracked labels** as section headers

### Hierarchy on Every Screen
1. **One number dominates** — the most important stat, in copper serif, large
2. **Context below** — who, what type ("visits left"), in secondary text
3. **Supporting data** — stats, grids, insights, separated by dividers
4. **Actions at bottom** — buttons, navigation, minimal

## 5. Signature Visual Moments

### The Onboarding Reveal
- Navy void, no texture, no grain
- "Think of someone you love." in 28pt serif light, bone white
- Text fields: bottom-border only (hairline), no backgrounds
- Gender/frequency selectors: copper fill when active
- **The reveal**: number appears in 120pt copper serif. Below: "visits left with Mom." in bone secondary. A hairline divider. Then: "This is what's left." in tertiary.
- Staggered animation: number at 0.8s, context at 2.0s, CTA at 3.5s
- The silence between elements IS the design

### The Dashboard
- Hero section: one person's name (ALL-CAPS tracked label) + their remaining visits in 48pt copper serif
- Stats row: two numbers with a vertical hairline divider between
- People list: rows separated by hairlines, percentage in serif, no cards
- No icons, no colored badges, no card decorations

### The Time Portrait (shareable)
- Full navy void background
- Name in ALL-CAPS tracked tertiary text
- Grid: past = near-invisible navy squares, future = copper squares
- Number in 72pt copper serif
- "visits left." in secondary text
- "TIMESLEFT" watermark in tertiary, barely visible
- **Share-worthy because it looks like nothing else** — stark, dark, one copper number

### The Life Calendar
- 4000+ tiny squares on navy void
- Lived weeks = near-invisible, remaining = bone white
- "Weeks left" number in copper serif
- Horizontal header with vertical divider between lived/remaining counts

---

## 6. What This Is NOT

- **Not warm/cozy** — the old amber/parchment/paper metaphor was comfort disguising the weight of the subject
- **Not hand-drawn** — programmatic wobble to fake imperfection is worse than clean geometry
- **Not decorated** — grain overlays, shadows, rounded corners, card borders all removed
- **Not multi-accent** — one accent color (copper), one meaning (time remaining)
- **Not light mode** — forced dark everywhere, the void is the identity

---

## 7. Design Test

For every design decision, ask:

> "If I showed this to someone and said 'AI made this,' would they believe me immediately?"

If yes, strip more away. The goal is design so restrained it could only be intentional.
